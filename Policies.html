Expense Policies

Clear guidelines for submitting and approving expenses to ensure transparency and compliance.

General Guidelines

All employees are expected to adhere to the following guidelines when submitting expense reports. Failure to comply may result in the rejection of the expense or disciplinary action.

Eligible Expenses

Eligible expenses include travel, accommodation, meals, and other business-related costs. All expenses must be necessary, reasonable, and directly related to company business. Personal expenses are not reimbursable.

Submission & Approval

Submission Process

Expense reports must be submitted within 30 days of the expense being incurred. Each expense must be itemized with a clear description, date, amount, and supporting documentation (e.g., receipts).

Approval Process

Expense reports will be reviewed and approved by the employee's manager. Managers are responsible for ensuring that all expenses comply with company policy. Approved expenses will be processed for reimbursement within 10 business days.

Specific Expense Categories

  • Travel Expenses: Must be pre-approved by the manager. Employees should choose the most economical options. Luxury accommodations or first-class travel will not be reimbursed without prior authorization.
  • Meal Expenses: Reimbursable up to a daily limit of $75. This includes meals with clients or during business travel. Itemized receipts are required.
  • Other Expenses: Costs like office supplies or conference fees must be pre-approved if they exceed $100. Receipts and a clear justification are required.

Policy Violations

Any expense report that violates company policy will be returned for correction. Repeated violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
